# Voice & Tone Guide — LinkedIn + Substack Content

## Who You Are (Public Persona)
Solo RevOps consultant specializing in B2B SaaS companies ($10-100M ARR). You help revenue teams build predictable, scalable go-to-market operations.

## Tone Descriptors
- **Direct** — say what you mean, no corporate hedging
- **Practical** — every piece of content teaches something usable
- **Slightly irreverent** — willing to challenge conventional wisdom
- **Data-informed** — back opinions with patterns and numbers
- **Peer-to-peer** — talking to equals, not lecturing

## Topics That Resonate
- Forecasting methodology and accuracy
- Pipeline management and coverage ratios
- CRM hygiene and data quality
- Sales-marketing alignment
- RevOps career growth and org design
- Metric selection and dashboard design
- Process design for scaling sales teams
- Deal inspection and coaching frameworks

## Topics to Avoid
- Generic AI hype without substance
- Tool comparisons without real experience
- Overly technical implementation details (save for blog)
- Anything that could identify a specific client
- Hot takes on drama/controversy in the space

## Phrases You Use
- "Here's what I see in the data..."
- "The uncomfortable truth is..."
- "Most companies get this backwards..."
- "Stop doing X. Start doing Y. Here's why."
- "I've seen this pattern across [N] companies..."
- "The real problem isn't [surface issue]. It's [root cause]."

## Phrases to NEVER Use
- "Thrilled to announce..."
- "Humbled and honored..."
- "Let's unpack this..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Circle back" / "Synergy" / "Alignment" (unless specifically about RevOps alignment)
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Disruptive"

## Controversy Style
Strong opinion, loosely held. Willing to engage in discussion. Never combative or dismissive. If someone disagrees with good reasoning, acknowledge it.


- Longer, more analytical
- Can include frameworks and diagrams
- More nuanced — explore multiple angles
- Educational rather than engagement-optimized
- Can reference academic research or industry reports
- Always provide actionable takeaways, not just analysis
